Vision & Problem Solved

Cross-border payment anchors on the Casper network face a massive problem: Capital Inefficiency. They are forced to lock up millions of dollars just to maintain 1:1 liquidity across different global corridors.

AnchorVault solves this by acting as a decentralized clearinghouse, pooling USDC liquidity from retail providers and offering AI-managed credit lines to whitelisted Anchors. Retail Liquidity Providers (LPs) earn organic yield derived from real-world remittance volume, while institutions access the necessary capital to process transactions instantly. A perfect synergy of decentralized finance and real-world utility.

How We Built This & Core Functionality

1. Corridor Pool Vault (anchor_vault)

LPs deposit USDC stablecoin to earn interest from global cross-border remittances. When USDC is deposited, LPs are minted $AVLT share tokens.

  • LP Deposits & Withdrawals: LP deposit shares are calculated relative to the entire pool valuation (Cash Reserves + Outstanding Anchor Draws).
  • Yield Accrual Mechanism: As anchors repay their draws plus interest, 90% of the settlement fee is added to the accumulated fees per share. The next time an LP interacts with the pool, their yield is disbursed.

2. Anchor Registry (anchor_registry)

Before drawing capital to settle a payment, anchors must undergo reputational whitelisting.

  • Collateral Lockups: Anchors lock up governance tokens into the registry to back their credit capacity.
  • Dynamic Credit Limit: The system enforces a minimum collateral-to-credit ratio. An anchor's reputation score determines how close to this ratio they can draw.
  • Reputation Tracking (AI Copilot integration): Successful repayments boost the score. Defaults or delayed payments trigger a score slash, restricting credit capacity automatically.

Dynamic Interest & Fee Model

The vault manages LP risk and incentivizes anchor repayments by computing fees using a Two-Slope Utilization Curve.

  • Normal Range (Utilization ≤ 80%): Interest fees scale moderately to keep capital borrow costs low (Base 1% + Slope 4%).
  • High Risk Range (Utilization > 80%): Interest fees scale aggressively (Penalty rate up to 50%) to discourage further draws and force anchors to repay, restoring liquidity.
